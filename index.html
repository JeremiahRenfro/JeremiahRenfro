Fighting games have been being made for a very long time since the 1980s. The first ever traditional fighting game is Street Fighter 1, but the genre got a boom after the release of Street FIghter 2. Then came Mortal Kombat which is the game that is responsible for the birth of the M rated games because of the games high violence.

Virtual Fighter released as the first 3D fighting game made where the fighters fight on a 3D plane which helped spawn a long lasting 3D fighting game series called Tekken which is clammerated as a fighting game series with the longest story.
While Virtual Fighter was the first 3D fighting game, Super Smash Bros for the Nintendo 64 was released as the first platform fighter. This helped create a sub genre of fighting games called platform fighters with the Super Smash Bros series being the most successful.
There was also the release of Melty Blood which acts as the first fast paced anime fighter.
Despite the fighting game genre's boom, it dissipated over time leading many to believe that the genre was dying. However, Capcom, the company that made Street Fighter, took a risk and released Street Fighter 4. Fortunately, the release of Street Fighter 4 has blossomed the fighting game genre and created another boom of fighting games. Nowadays, the fighting game genre is still thriving with the upcoming releases of Street Fighter 6 and Tekken 8 as will as more fighting games soon to come.
