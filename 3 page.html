Assuming you are curious about how to play these kinds of games, this will show you various basics you need to be aware of before hopping into a fighting game.

Blocking

In traditional fighting games, you typically have to move the control stick or d-pad in the opposite direction of your opponent to block their upcoming attacks (sometimes you can press a button to block to). However, there are 2 attacks to keep in mind, and those two attacks are low attacks and overhead attacks.



Low Attacks

The low attacks are attacks a fighter does that hits their opponent at the lower body. When they do this, you have to move the direction down diagonally in the opposite direction of your opponent, or crouch, to block the low attack (doing this would also still block normal attacks as well).

Overhead Attacks

The overhead attacks are attacks a fighter does that attacks at the most top of the body. This is used against opponents that are crouching to block low-attacks so when you notice an overhead coming, you have to stand up and block normally like you would against a normal attack. NOTE: Aerial attacks, the attacks from the fighter in the air, have the same function as overhead attacks, so you have to stand up against those as well.

Motion Inputs

The one thing that beginner players will have trouble getting used to are motion inputs. These are the inputs made from the movement of your control stick/d-pad. To get a good grasp of what they are like, here are the most common and basic inputs you need to know about.

Forward

Back

Quarter Circle

Dragon Uppercut

Half Circle

The direction of these motion inputs varies between which side of the screen your fighter is facing and what motion inputs a character has available that you can use. For example, a character may only use quarter circle and dragon uppercut inputs, but a different character may have those same inputs except they themselves have the half circle input that the other characters do not have.
